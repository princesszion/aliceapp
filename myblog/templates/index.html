About Me

I am a development practitioner and education specialist with over ten years of experience designing and implementing high-impact programs in the not-for-profit sector across Nigeria and Rwanda. My expertise spans Education Quality, Child Protection and Safeguarding. I have led and managed cross-functional teams across Africa, leveraging my deep understanding of program design, stakeholder engagement, and impact-driven implementation to drive sustainable change.

Beyond program management, I am an experienced coach, mentor, facilitator, and public speaker. Through my training sessions, I equip education leaders in both the public and private sectors with the skills and strategies needed to transform learning environments, enhance instructional leadership, and drive systemic change. My expertise also extends to teacher training and capacity building,helping educators align with the vision of government and school proprietors to foster a culture of excellence and continuous improvement in education.

My professional interests lie within the following areas:

  • Trauma Informed care

  • Adolescent counselling and Coaching

  • Child abuse and neglect

  • Behaviour Therapy

  • Family life

  • Values and Virtue education

  • Character Development and Purpose Education

  • Teacher Education

  • Usable knowledge and 21st Century skills

  • Program and Instructional Design

Everything rises and falls on the family

My Work

John Maxwell is famous for the saying ‘Everything rises and falls on Leadership’. I agree totally and add to this that everything rises and falls on parenting and family life. Everyone comes from a family, the family is the bedrock of the society, if we get things right at the home front then we have little left to do as society.

I started my career with Voluntary Service Overseas (VSO) as a Volunteer in Ikorodu and later became a Team Lead in Gwada community in Nigeria. In this role, I created awareness among parents and stakeholders on the ills of child abuse and neglect, animated inclusive neighbourhood spaces for children, equipped children with knowledge on rights and responsibilities, and carried out baseline surveys and action research.

As the Co-Founder of Wit21 Hub, I spearhead initiatives that equip children and teenagers with future-ready skills, promote child safeguarding, and advance educational innovation. My work includes curriculum development to ensure students are prepared for the future, integrating technology and AI in education, and shaping education policies that drive long-term impact. Additionally, I support organizations in designing and managing education-focused CSR initiatives that align with their mission and contribute meaningfully to community development.

Over the last 5 years, I have been at the forefront of building the following organisations and key initiatives across Africa.

  • Wit21 Hub

  • ToddlerLearning360

  • The Godward Teen

  • JustBe Podcast

  • Midark Rwanda

  • Writing Hub

Books

Coming Soon!!
Be on the lookout for my latest book: A Teen’s Search for Meaning

A Teen's Search for Meaning is an essential guide for teenagers seeking to uncover their life's purpose and navigate the challenges of adolescence with intention and clarity. This insightful book empowers teens to avoid the pitfalls of frivolity by offering practical advice on developing a strong vision, crafting a meaningful life mission, and choosing friends who uplift and support their journey. Through real-life examples, engaging exercises, and thought-provoking reflections, Alice provides teens with the tools they need to save time, make informed choices, and embark on a path of self-discovery and fulfilment. "A Teen's Search for Meaning" is a must-read for any young person determined to maximize their potential and lead a purposeful life.
